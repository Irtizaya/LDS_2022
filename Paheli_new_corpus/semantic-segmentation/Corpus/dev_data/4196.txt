:: J U D G M E N T :	PREAMBLE
: The Police Inspector of Women Police Station, Mysuru city has filed the charge sheet against accused for the offences punishable under sections 498(A) R/w section 34 of I.P.C., Section 3 and 4 of Dowry Prohibition Act. 2.	FAC
The brief facts of the case of the prosecution are that:- The complainant and accused No.1 married on 09.07.2009 before the Sub-Registrar at Mysuru and thereafter the said marriage was performed before the elders on 20.09.2010 at Kendaganneshwara temple.	FAC
It is alleged in the complaint that the accused demanded for Rs.10,00,000/- and gold ornaments.	FAC
The parents of the complainant gave Rs.10,00,000/- and other gold ornaments as per the demand.	FAC
It is alleged that the accused No.1 to 3 started harassing the complainant for one or the other reason.	FAC
The accused abused the complainant in filthy language.	FAC
The complainant has alleged that the accused subjected her for physical assault.	FAC
The accused demanded for further dowry amount from the complainant.	FAC
Even after receipt of Rs.2,00,000/- after the marriage the accused did not bother to take care of the complainant.	FAC
It is also alleged that the accused demanded money for the purchase of Zen Car.	FAC
The accused used to come to the place of work of the complainant and pick up quarrel with her.	FAC
The complainant was driven out of the house and the gold and silver articles and the amount which was received as dowry by the accused was not returned to the complainant.	FAC
Hence the complaint is lodged by the complainant against the accused No.1 to 4 before the concerned police.	FAC
CW.12 has registered the case against the accused.	FAC
CW.13 has further conducted the investigation and filed the charge sheet against the accused No.1 to 4 for offences punishable U/s.498(A) R/w section 34 of I.P.C. and Section 3 and 4 of Dowry Prohibition Act.	FAC
 3. The accused No.1 to 4 are on Court bail.	FAC
The charge framed, read over and explained to the accused.	FAC
After understanding the charge the accused denied the same and claimed to be tried.	FAC
 4. The prosecution has examined PW.1 to 3 and the documents are marked as Ex.P1 to P4.	FAC
As there is no incriminating evidence against the accused, the statement of accused U/s.313 of Cr.P.C. is dispensed with.	ANALYSIS
 5. Heard arguments of Learned Assistant Public Prosecutor and counsel for accused.	NONE
6. The points that emerge for consideration are:-	ISSUE
1) Whether the prosecution proves beyond reasonable doubt that the accused No.1 being the husband of CW.1 in furtherance of common intention has ill treated the complainant and the accused No.1 to 3 started harassing the complainant and abused the complainant in filthy language and he subjected her for physical assault also even after receipt of Rs.2,00,000/- after the marriage the accused did not bother to take care of the complainant and the accused used to come to the place of work of the complainant and pick up quarrel with her, the complainant was driven out of the house and thereby committed an offence punishable under section 498(A) R/w section 34 of I.P.C.?	ISSUE
 2) Whether the prosecution further proves beyond reasonable doubt that at the time of marriage, the accused demanded for Rs.10,00,000/- and gold ornaments and the parents of the complainant gave Rs.10,00,000/- and other gold ornaments as per the demand and thereby committed an offence punishable under section 3 of Dowry Prohibition Act?	ISSUE
 3) Whether the prosecution further proves beyond reasonable doubt that the accused harassed the complainant physically and mentally in demand of money and Even after receipt of Rs.2,00,000/- after the marriage the accused did not bother to take care of the complainant and the accused demanded money for the purchase of Zen Car and thereby committed an offence punishable under section 4 of Dowry Prohibition Act?	ISSUE
 4) What Order?	ISSUE
 7. Perused oral and documentary evidence placed on record.	NONE
 Having heard, the answer to above points are:- Point No. 1 :  In the Negative Point No. 2 : Point No. 3 :  In the Negative Point No. 4 : In the Negative As per final order below, for the following : R E A S O N S : 8. POINT No.1 TO 3 :- The complainant, Smt. Nayana and her parents are examined on behalf of the prosecution as PW.1 to 3.	ANALYSIS
 9. The contents of the complaint are that the complainant and the accused No.1 married on 09.07.2009 before the Sub-Registrar, Mysuru and thereafter the marriage was performed before the elders on 20.09.2010.	ANALYSIS
It is alleged in the complaint that the complainant was tortured physically and mentally in demand of money by the accused No.1 to 4.	ANALYSIS
The accused demanded for money to open a shop and a Gym center.	ANALYSIS
Even after compliance of the dowwry the accused harassed the complainant.	ANALYSIS
The accused demanded for Zen Car.	ANALYSIS
The accused also caused harassment to the complainant in her work place.	ANALYSIS
The evidence of PW.1 is totally contrary to the complaint.	ANALYSIS
PW.1 has not deposed in accordance with the contents of the complaint.	ANALYSIS
When the complainant turned hostile and not supported the case of the prosecution she was cross examined by the learned APP.	ANALYSIS
10. The PW.1 has admitted during the cross examination about the compromise held in between the complainant and the accused.	ANALYSIS
 11. PW.2 Sri. Girigowda is the father of the complainant who turned hostile and not supported the case of the prosecution.	ANALYSIS
 11. PW.3 Smt. Pramila is the mother of the complainant who turned hostile and not supported the case of the prosecution.	ANALYSIS
 12. The learned APP prayed to issue summons to the other witnesses, but no purpose will be served in examining the other witnesses in view of the oral evidence given by the complainant before the Court of law.	ANALYSIS
Hence, the prayer of learned APP was rejected and the case of the prosecution was closed.	ANALYSIS
As there was no incriminating evidence the statement of accused U/s.313 of Cr.P.C. is also dispensed with.	RATIO
 13. As per Sec.498(A) of I.P.C. husband or the relative of the husband who cause any ill treatment to the aggrieved woman are liable for the alleged offences.	STA
But in view of the evidence of PW.1 to 3, the contents of the complaint are not supported.	RATIO
Therefore, the prosecution has failed to prove the guilt of the accused No.1 to 4 U/s.498(A) R/w section 34 of I.P.C., Section 3 and 4 of Dowry Prohibition Act.	RATIO
Therefore, the accused No.1 to 4 are entitle for acquittal.	RATIO
Hence, I answer Point No.1 to 5 in the Negative.	NONE
 14. POINT No.6 :	RPC
- In the result I proceed to pass the following : :: O R D E R :: Acting U/s.248(1) of Cr.P.C. the accused No.1 to 4 are acquitted for the offences punishable under sections 498(A) R/w section 34 of I.P.C. and section 3 and 4 of Dowry Prohibition Act.                           	RPC
The bail bonds of the accused No.1 to 4 and their sureties stand cancelled.	RPC
